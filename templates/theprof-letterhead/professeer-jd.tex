\jdsection{About the role}
We turn raw court-case data into intelligence. As a full-stack technical intern, you'll work directly with our engineering team across ETL pipelines that ingest our data to the interfaces that make it useful. Start anytime from July onwards, with a minimum commitment of two months.

\jdsection{What we're looking for}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Currently in final year of an undergraduate or masters program (CS, Engineering, Data Science, Economics or related)
  \item Solid programming fundamentals in Python; comfort with JavaScript/TypeScript is a plus
  \item At least one self-initiated project outside of regular coursework that you can walk us through (personal, papers, hackathon, freelance etc.)
  \item Self-directed with clear written communication skills
\end{itemize}

\jdsection{Nice to have}
\begin{itemize}
  \item You're curious about more than just tech. Our team wears many hats — one lunch we argue over politics, the next over fashion — and even over a screen, we'd love someone who jumps into those kind of conversations.
\end{itemize}

\jdsection{What we offer}
\begin{itemize}
  \item ₹15k a month + a subscription to Claude or Codex for the period.
  \item A real shot at a full-time role. We see this internship as a way to find people we want to build with long-term.
\end{itemize}

\jdsection{How to apply}
Email your CV to \href{mailto:careers@theprofesseer.com}{\textcolor{profCrimson}{\underline{careers@theprofesseer.com}}}, and include a link to the project you'd most like to walk us through, your earliest start date, and how long you can commit.
